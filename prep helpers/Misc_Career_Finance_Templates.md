### 2.4 Counter-Offer Email Templates

**Template 1: Initial Counter**
```
Subject: Re: Offer Letter — [Role] at [Company]

Hi [HR/Recruiter],

Thank you for the offer! I'm genuinely excited about joining [Company] 
and the [specific team/project] — it aligns perfectly with my goals.

After reviewing the package, I'd like to discuss the compensation. 
Based on my research (levels.fyi, market data) and considering my 
OSCP certification, ML security expertise, and [X] years of 
specialized experience, I was expecting a total comp closer to 
₹[X+20-30%] LPA.

Would there be flexibility to bridge this gap — whether through 
base adjustment, signing bonus, or additional RSUs?

I'm very keen to make this work. Happy to discuss further.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

**Template 2: When They Say "This is Final"**
```
Hi [HR],

I understand budget constraints. A few alternatives that might work:
1. Signing bonus of ₹[X]L (one-time, easier to approve)
2. Accelerated review at 6 months instead of 12
3. Education budget of ₹[X]L/year (for OMSCS + certifications)
4. Additional [X] RSUs with standard vesting
5. Higher designation (Senior vs Mid) — helps my long-term trajectory

Any combination of these would help close the gap. What's feasible?
```

**Template 3: Playing Multiple Offers**
```
Hi [HR],

product, growth path]. However, the ₹[gap]L difference is significant.

Is there any way to match or come closer? I'd love to make this work 
with [Company A] as my first choice.

### 2.5 Questions to Ask Before Accepting

```
MUST-ASK QUESTIONS:

Compensation:
□ What's the RSU/ESOP vesting schedule? (1-year cliff? 4-year vest?)
□ What's the refresher grant policy? (annual additional RSU grants?)
□ What's the bonus payout history? (target % vs actual %)
□ Is there a retention/loyalty bonus?
□ What's the policy on counter-offers? (will they match if I get external?)

Growth:
□ What does the promotion timeline look like? (L4→L5 typical timeline?)
□ What's the team structure? Who would I report to?
□ What projects would I work on in first 6 months?
□ Is there a path to [specific role I want]?
□ Do you support conference attendance? (paid travel + tickets?)

Logistics:
□ What's the notice period? (can it be 30 days instead of 90?)
□ WFH policy? (how many days remote per week?)
□ What's the relocation package? (if moving cities)
□ Joining date flexibility?
□ Background verification timeline?

Red Flag Answers:
❌ "We'll discuss RSU at your first review" (means no RSU now)
❌ "Bonus is variable" without clear % target
❌ "Promotion depends on business needs" (no clear path)
❌ 90-day notice period (try to negotiate to 60 or 30)
❌ "We don't do counter-offers" (may be true, may be pressure tactic)
```

### 3.7 Key Google Sheets Formulas Reference

```
ESSENTIAL FORMULAS FOR YOUR TRACKER:

1. SAVINGS RATE:
   =Investment_Total/Income_Total*100

2. XIRR (True SIP return):
   =XIRR(B2:B50, A2:A50)  [B=cashflows, A=dates]
   Note: First SIP as negative, current value as positive last entry

3. LIVE STOCK PRICE:
   =GOOGLEFINANCE("NSE:INFY", "price")
   =GOOGLEFINANCE("NASDAQ:AAPL", "price")

4. CURRENCY CONVERSION:
   =GOOGLEFINANCE("CURRENCY:USDINR")

5. SIP FUTURE VALUE:
   =FV(12%/12, 120, -10000, 0, 1)
   (₹10K/month, 12% return, 10 years = ₹23.2L)

6. SIP NEEDED FOR GOAL:
   =PMT(12%/12, 60, 0, -5000000)
   (₹50L goal, 12% return, 5 years = ₹61,200/month needed)

7. COMPOUND GROWTH:
   =PV * (1 + rate)^years
   =100000 * (1+0.12)^10  → ₹3.1L (₹1L at 12% for 10 years)

8. RULE OF 72:
   =72/Annual_Return_Percent  → Years to double
   72/12 = 6 years to double at 12%

9. CONDITIONAL FORMATTING:
   Savings Rate ≥50% → Green
   Savings Rate 30-50% → Yellow
   Savings Rate <30% → Red

10. NET WORTH CHART:
    Select monthly net worth column → Insert → Chart → Line Chart
    Shows your wealth growth trajectory visually
```

```

├── ITR-2: Salary + capital gains (mutual funds, stocks, crypto)

├── Login with PAN + password
├── Go to: e-File → Income Tax Returns → File Income Tax Return

├── Click "Pre-fill" → system auto-populates from Form 26AS
├── Verify: Salary details match Form 16
├── Verify: TDS deducted matches
├── Verify: Bank interest (savings account) is included
└── Add any missing income (freelance, other sources)

├── Enter as per Form 16 Part B:
│   ├── Gross salary
│   ├── Allowances (HRA, LTA, special)
│   ├── Perquisites
│   ├── Less: Standard deduction
│   └── Net taxable salary
└── Cross-check with Form 16 — numbers must match exactly

STEP 4: Other Income
├── Savings bank interest (add all bank accounts)
├── FD interest (check Form 26AS — bank reports this)
├── Dividend income (from stocks/mutual funds)
├── Any other income (freelance, gifts > ₹50K, etc.)
└── Interest from IT refund (if you got refund last year)

STEP 5: Capital Gains (if you sold MF/stocks)
├── Short-term (held < 1 year for stocks, < 1 year for equity MF)

│   ├── Groww: Reports → Tax Report
│   ├── CAMS/KFintech: Consolidated statement

│   ├── 80E: Education loan interest
│   ├── 80G: Donations to approved charities

│   └── Only standard deduction + NPS employer contribution

├── System auto-calculates tax
├── Verify against your Form 16 tax computation
├── Check TDS already deducted (should show credit)
├── If refund due: Verify bank account (pre-validated)
├── If tax due: Pay via challan before filing

STEP 8: Verification (IMPORTANT — return is INVALID without this!)

□ PPF: Passbook screenshot / deposit receipt
□ ELSS: Mutual fund statement showing investment
□ LIC: Premium paid receipt
□ Tuition fees: Fee receipt from institution

80D (₹25K self + ₹25K parents):
□ Health insurance: Policy + premium receipt
□ Preventive health checkup: Receipt (₹5K included in 80D)

HRA:
□ Rent agreement (registered if > ₹20K/month in some states)
□ Rent receipts (monthly, with revenue stamps)
□ Landlord PAN (mandatory if rent > ₹1,00,000/year)
□ Landlord declaration (if no PAN available)

80CCD(1B) — NPS:
□ NPS transaction statement showing ₹50K contribution

80E — Education Loan:
□ Interest certificate from bank/NBFC
□ (Only interest is deductible, not principal)

80G — Donations:
□ Receipt from registered charity with 80G registration number
□ (100% or 50% deduction depending on organization)
```

### 6.6 Common Tax Mistakes to Avoid

```
MISTAKES YOUNG PROFESSIONALS MAKE:

1. NOT FILING RETURN (even if zero tax)
   ├── Problem: No ITR = can't get loan, visa, or credit card
   ├── Fix: Always file even if refund is ₹0
   └── Penalty: ₹5,000 if filed after July 31 (₹1,000 if income < ₹5L)

2. FORGETTING BANK INTEREST
   ├── Problem: IT department has this data — mismatch triggers notice
   ├── Fix: Report ALL savings account + FD interest
   └── Check Form 26AS/AIS — everything reported there must be in ITR

3. NOT REPORTING MUTUAL FUND GAINS
   ├── Problem: Even if you reinvest, selling/switching = taxable event
   ├── Fix: Report STCG/LTCG from equity + debt funds
   └── Even LTCG up to ₹1.25L (exempt) must be REPORTED in ITR

4. WRONG REGIME SELECTION
   ├── Problem: Choosing old regime without enough deductions = more tax
   ├── Fix: Calculate both, pick lower. New regime is default.
   └── You can switch regime every year (as salaried employee)

5. NOT VERIFYING ITR (e-verification)
   ├── Problem: Unverified return = treated as NOT FILED
   ├── Fix: Verify via Aadhaar OTP within 30 days
   └── Takes 30 seconds. No excuse.

6. MISSING FORM 16 PART B DETAILS
   ├── Problem: Copy-paste errors in salary breakup
   ├── Fix: Use pre-fill feature — auto-populates from employer data
   └── Cross-verify: Pre-filled data vs Form 16 numbers

7. NOT CLAIMING HRA (living in rented accommodation)
   ├── Problem: Losing ₹50K-1.5L in deductions
   ├── Fix: Keep rent receipts, get landlord PAN
   └── Even paying rent to parents is valid (they report as income)
```

### 8.4 Getting Your First Clients (Cold Start Problem)

```
THE FIRST CLIENT IS THE HARDEST. Here's the exact playbook:

WEEK 1-2: SETUP
□ Create profiles on: Fiverr + Upwork + HackerOne
□ Write compelling bio (see template below)
□ Set up portfolio: GitHub projects + 2-3 sample reports
□ Create 3 "gig" packages on Fiverr (see pricing section)
□ Take Upwork skill tests (Top 10% badge helps)

WEEK 3-4: FIRST CLIENTS (accept low rates to build reviews)
□ Strategy 1 — Fiverr (easiest first sale):
   ├── Create gig: "I will do a security audit of your website"
   ├── Price LOW to start: ₹2-5K (you need those first 5 reviews)
   ├── Deliver EXCEPTIONAL quality (overdeliver on first 5 clients)
   └── Once 5★ reviews: Raise prices 2-3x
□ Strategy 2 — Upwork proposals (10/day):
   ├── Apply to EVERY relevant job (security, Python, automation)
   ├── Personalize each proposal (never copy-paste)
   ├── Mention something specific from their job description
   ├── Offer a small free sample or insight (shows competence)
   └── Success rate: 1 in 20 proposals wins a job (normal)
□ Strategy 3 — Bug bounties (no client needed):
   ├── Start with VDPs (Vulnerability Disclosure Programs — no bounty but builds rep)
   ├── Target: 1 valid bug in first month
   ├── Focus on: IDOR, XSS, information disclosure (easiest to find)
   └── Hall of fame mentions → proof for clients
□ Strategy 4 — LinkedIn outreach:
   ├── Post 3x/week about security topics (build authority)
   ├── DM startup founders: "I noticed [specific vulnerability/concern]
   │   on your site. Happy to help fix it — here's my portfolio."
   ├── DO NOT spam. Be genuinely helpful. Give value first.
   └── 1 in 10 conversations → paid engagement

MONTH 2-3: BUILDING MOMENTUM
□ Ask every happy client for:
   ├── 5★ review on the platform
   ├── LinkedIn recommendation
   ├── Referral to other companies
   └── Repeat work / retainer possibility
□ Raise rates after every 5 completed projects
□ Start turning down low-paying work (your time has more value now)
□ Create case studies from completed projects (anonymized)
```

### 8.6 Pricing Strategy

```
PRICING PRINCIPLES:
├── Hourly is for starting. Move to PROJECT-BASED ASAP.
├── Project pricing = more money for same work (you get faster over time)
├── Value-based pricing: Charge based on VALUE to client, not your time
├── Example: Security audit preventing a ₹50L breach → charge ₹2L, not ₹20K
└── NEVER compete on price alone (race to bottom = burnout)

STARTER RATES (first 3-6 months — building reviews):
├── Security audit (small site): ₹5-15K
├── Bug bounty (platform rewards): ₹5-50K/bug
├── Python script/automation: ₹5-10K
├── Technical article: ₹3-5K
├── ML model (basic): ₹15-25K
└── Hourly (Upwork): $15-25/hr (₹1,250-2,100/hr)

EXPERIENCED RATES (6-12 months — good reviews, portfolio):
├── Security audit (small-medium): ₹25-75K
├── Pentest (full application): ₹50K-2L
├── Security consulting (retainer): ₹30-50K/month
├── Python/ML project: ₹30-80K
├── Technical writing: ₹8-15K/article

### 8.8 Tax & Legal for Freelance Income

```
LEGAL STRUCTURE:

OPTION 1: Individual (simplest — start here)
├── No registration needed for income < ₹20L/year
├── File ITR-3 or ITR-4 (instead of ITR-1)
├── Pay tax on freelance income as "business income"
├── Deduct expenses (laptop, internet, software, courses)
└── Best for: Side income < ₹20L/year

OPTION 2: GST Registration (required if > ₹20L/year)
├── Register when freelance income crosses ₹20L/year
├── Charge 18% GST to clients (you deposit to govt)
├── Claim input tax credit on business purchases
├── File monthly/quarterly GST returns (or hire CA for ₹5-10K/year)
└── For international clients: "Export of services" = 0% GST (but still register)

OPTION 3: LLP / Company (when freelance > full-time salary)
├── Liability protection
├── More deductions available
├── Looks more professional to enterprise clients
├── Cost: ₹10-20K to set up + ₹15-30K/year compliance
└── Best for: Freelance income > ₹10L/year consistently

TAX ON FREELANCE INCOME:
├── Freelance income = "Profits and Gains from Business/Profession"
├── Option A — Normal computation:
│   ├── Income - Expenses = Profit → taxed at slab rate
│   └── Maintain books of accounts (invoice register, expense receipts)
├── Option B — Presumptive taxation (Section 44ADA):
│   ├── Declare 50% of gross receipts as profit (no need to track expenses)
│   ├── Available if gross receipts < ₹75L/year
│   ├── Much simpler — no detailed books needed
│   └── Tax only on the 50% "deemed profit"
│   └── Example: Earn ₹6L freelance → Profit = ₹3L → Add to salary for tax
└── Advance tax: If tax liability > ₹10,000, pay quarterly advance tax

DEDUCTIBLE EXPENSES (reduces your tax):
├── Laptop / computer (depreciation)
├── Internet bill (50% if shared personal/business)
├── Software subscriptions (Burp Suite, cloud, tools)
├── Online courses / certifications (business skill development)
├── Co-working space membership
├── Phone bill (30-50% business use)
├── Travel for client meetings
├── Professional books and resources
└── CA/accountant fees

PAYMENT & INVOICING:
├── International clients: Use Wise / Payoneer / PayPal (Wise best rates)
├── Indian clients: Bank transfer / UPI / Razorpay payment link
├── Always send invoice (even informal) — proof of income
├── Keep separate bank account for freelance (easier accounting)
└── Save 30% of every freelance payment for tax (auto-transfer to savings)
```

### 8.11 Client Red Flags & Protection

```
RED FLAGS — WALK AWAY IF:
├── 🚩 "We'll pay after we get funding" (= never)
├── 🚩 "Can you do a free trial project?" (= free work extraction)
├── 🚩 "Budget is very low but it'll lead to bigger projects" (rarely true)
├── 🚩 Won't sign contract or pay upfront (= plan to not pay)
├── 🚩 Scope keeps expanding without extra payment ("scope creep")
├── 🚩 Rude/disrespectful communication (won't get better)
├── 🚩 Wants you available 24/7 (this is freelance, not employment)
├── 🚩 "Just hack this site for me" with no proof of ownership (ILLEGAL)
├── 🚩 Asks for personal info/banking beyond payment details
└── 🚩 Multiple freelancers complain about them (check Upwork reviews)

PROTECTION STRATEGIES:
├── ALWAYS get 50% upfront (non-negotiable for new clients)
├── Use platform escrow (Upwork/Fiverr holds money until you deliver)
├── Written scope: If it's not in the contract, you're not doing it
├── Milestone payments for large projects (split into 3-4 payments)
├── Keep all communication in writing (email/platform chat, not just calls)
├── Screenshot important agreements
├── Never start work without signed authorization (for pentesting)
├── Weekly updates: Keep client informed (prevents surprise disputes)
└── Document hours: Even on project-based, track your time for reference

HANDLING SCOPE CREEP:
├── Client asks for more work beyond original scope
├── Response: "Happy to help with that! It's outside our original scope,
│   so I'll send a quick quote for the additional work. Shall I proceed?"
├── Be firm but friendly — this is business, not charity
├── If client pushes back: "Our agreement covers [X]. The new request
│   is [Y], which requires [Z] additional hours. Here are the options..."
└── Never do extra work hoping they'll pay later — they won't
```
